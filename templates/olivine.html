About Olivine

Olivine is a common mineral in the Earth's mantle and is often found in igneous rocks. This analysis shows the reflectance spectra measured using the ASD spectrometer, separated into Absolute Reflectance (AREF) and Relative Reflectance (RREF) measurements.

Spectral Analysis

The plot displays individual sample measurements as thin lines, with the mean spectrum shown as a thick colored line. AREF represents direct reflectance measurements, while RREF shows reflectance relative to a reference standard. This data comes from the USGS ASD Spectrometer catalogue and can be used for mineral identification and classification.

Technical Details

  • Instrument: ASD FieldSpec Spectrometer
  • Wavelength Range: 0.35-2.5 micrometers (µm)
  • Data Points: 2,151 per spectrum
  • Measurement Types: AREF (Absolute) and RREF (Relative)
